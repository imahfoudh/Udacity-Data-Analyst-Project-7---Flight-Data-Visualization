Analysis of US Flight Data for 2018-2019 from Bureau of Transportation Statistics¶

by (Mohammed Almahfoudh)¶

Investigation Overview¶

The purpose of this project is to invistigate the data on flights in the US for two years period (2018-2019), and to drive an insight for a number of factors including:

  • Overall flight activity
  • Flight cancellation
  • Flight delays
  • Flight operators

Dataset Overview¶

The data was extracted from the Bureau of Transportation Statistics database, and includes 29 data columns similar to the ones used for the Statistical Computing Statistical Graphics in Data Expo 09.

2. Flight Durations Distribution¶

After looking at the carriers, we looked at the distribution of the flights duration in the slected period. We can see from the chart that most of the flights last for less than 200 minutes, which can be explained by that two of the top 5 airlines in flight count do short trips and are economical in nature. (Flights lasting longer than 500 minutes are excluded)

5. Flight Cancellation Across Months/Years in Relation to Cancellation Type¶

Since there are different cancellation types provided, it we wanted to look at the cancellation pattern for each type for every year. The chart shows an interesting pattern, while Cancellation Code A (Carrier) does not appear to follow a mutual distribution between the years, the Code B (Weather) ones are interestingly similar with small changes (maybe due to climate change?). Code C cancellations (National Air System) is also following a similar trend in both years.

6. Flight Distance-Duration Plot Across the Years for Top 5 Airlines by Traffic¶

Looking at the distance/flight time relationship for the top-5 airlines, both years have almost identical pattern. The chart showed the way the airlines operate in the US. we could see how Southwest distance is limited to approx 3000 miles, while SkyWest airlines is even shorter, approx 2000 miles. This could be related to the fleet type they operate with.
